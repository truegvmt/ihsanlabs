## Vision

Ihsan Labs is the OpenAI for philanthropy — a frictionless intelligence platform that helps Muslims allocate charity and waqf funds to the highest-impact opportunities available, verified by evidence, optimised for long-term barakah, and executable in a single tap.

The product is built on a simple truth: most Muslims want to give well but face a credibility gap. They cannot easily distinguish between a transparent, high-impact organisation and one that absorbs donations without producing verifiable benefit. Ihsan closes that gap by doing the verification work silently, before the donor ever sees a recommendation.

The experience must feel like consulting a trusted, knowledgeable friend — not filling out a form, not reading a dashboard.

## Brand

**Name:** Ihsan Labs

**Root word:** *iḥsān* (إحسان) — to do something with full excellence and with the awareness that Allah sees. The highest register of Islamic ethics. Not merely doing good, but doing it with care, precision, and sincerity.

**Positioning:** The OpenAI for philanthropy. The cleanest, most intelligent giving tool available to Muslim donors globally.

**Tone:** Quiet confidence. Reverent without being preachy. Evidence-based without being clinical. Dignified without being cold.

**Typography system:**
- Display / headings: Cormorant Garamond (serif, light weight, high elegance)
- UI / body: Instrument Sans (clean, contemporary)
- Data / metadata / receipts: DM Mono (technical precision)

**Colour palette:**
- Background: Parchment `#f5f0e8` — warm, grounded, not sterile
- Primary ink: `#0f0e0c` — near-black, not pure black
- Gold accent: `#c4962a` — earned, not decorative; used for scores, highlights, and calls to action
- Moss: `#2a3d2e` — used for high-stakes cards (Living Waqf, confirmation)
- Sand: `#e8dfc8` — secondary borders and dividers

**What we never do:** Purple gradients. Rounded pill buttons everywhere. Charity stock photography. Emoji in body copy. Superlatives ("amazing", "incredible", "life-changing").

## The Donor Journey

The full journey from intention to living waqf takes under 3 minutes on first use and under 90 seconds on repeat use. It has five named steps.

### Step 1 — Intention Tap (target: 15 seconds)

The donor opens the app or site and sees a single screen with four choice clusters: focus (water, education, food, healthcare, mosque, orphan care), time horizon (one gift, monthly, forever/waqf), region (country pin or global), and budget. Below the toggles is a single open-text field with the prompt: *"Why are you giving right now?"* The donor types or selects three words.

This screen must feel like an invitation, not a form. The UI is minimal and unhurried. No progress bars. No explanatory copy beyond the field labels.

The intention vector generated from these inputs is stored in the donor's profile and used for all future personalisation. Anonymous sessions are fully supported — no account is required for Phase 0.

### Step 2 — Resonance Preview (target: under 2 seconds to render)

The system returns a single allocation plan: one primary project (85% of budget) and one diversification project (15%), with a barakah score (0–100), an evocative line of twelve words or fewer describing the project's long-term impact, and the allocation split shown as a visual bar. The evocative line is generated by the LLM using Prompt A and grounded in retrieved field evidence — it is never generic.

Example evocative line: *"Water rising from the earth, season after season."*

The donor has two choices from this screen: **Give now** (goes immediately to Fast Approval) or **Explain** (opens the Due Diligence panel inline, below the allocation card). The Explain path does not interrupt the flow — the allocation card stays visible above.

### Step 3 — Guided Reflection (optional, target: 60 seconds)

Before giving, the donor may choose a 60-second reflection. This is a short audio script — calm, second-person, spiritually grounded — that connects the donor's stated intention to the project's impact and closes with a dua template they can share with family. The script is generated by the LLM using the same Prompt A output that produced the evocative line.

This step is entirely optional and skippable. Its purpose is conversion: donors who engage with the reflection give more consistently and return at higher rates.

### Step 4 — Fast Approval (target: 10 seconds)

A clean summary of the allocation split and total amount, with payment method options (Stripe card, bank transfer, and in future: crypto and recurring waqf escrow). One confirmation tap. A cryptographic receipt is generated immediately: SHA-256 hash of the payment data, stored in the `audit_log` table and displayed to the donor as a text string they can save.

There are no extra screens, no upsells, no "before you go" prompts.

### Step 5 — Living Waqf Card (ongoing, lifetime)

Immediately after confirmation, the donor's Living Waqf Card is activated. This is a minimalist card — available as a mobile widget and on the donor's dashboard — that shows: the waqf asset name and location, a serenity score (0–100, derived from telemetry and field reports), the current beneficiary count, the next scheduled maintenance date, and one human-sentence update from the most recent field report.

Updates arrive as single-sentence push notifications, at most weekly. Each notification is composed by the LLM (Prompt D) from raw event data, keeping it specific and personal. If the donor taps "Show raw evidence," they see the exact PDF excerpt or sensor reading that generated the update.

The card is permanent. It lives in the donor's profile indefinitely and continues reporting for as long as the waqf asset is operational.

## The Due Diligence Panel

The Due Diligence panel is the trust layer. It opens inline when the donor taps "Explain" and shows:

A score ring displaying the project's final score (deterministic base + LLM adjustment, 0–100). A two-to-three sentence plain-English summary of what the evidence confirms and what is uncertain. A risk list of up to five items, each tagged with a severity level (low / medium / high / critical) and the specific evidence gap or confirmed concern behind it. A maintenance plan note. Three cited sources, each linking to the original document with its PDF hash visible.

The panel is generated by the LLM using Prompt B and cached for seven days. On repeat views it loads in under 200ms. The donor can always see the raw evidence behind any cited claim.

## The Donor Journal

Once per year, the system sends a gentle prompt: *"What did this waqf teach you this year?"* The donor types one or two lines. The system generates a short spiritual narrative (Prompt E) connecting their year of giving to its documented impact, along with a dua snippet suitable for sharing at a gathering. The journal is private by default and exportable as a formatted PDF.

---

## The Waqf Agent

For donors who opt in, the autonomous waqf agent monitors real-time signals — conflict alerts, infrastructure outages, price shocks — and proposes or automatically executes small reallocation decisions within donor-defined policy limits. The agent never exceeds the donor's declared contingency balance and never acts without a cited signal. Actions requiring confirmation arrive as a single-tap approval notification. All agent actions are logged immutably.

---

## What the Product Does Not Do

It does not store unnecessary personal data. It does not show advertising. It does not create urgency through countdown timers or "X donors gave today" social proof. It does not recommend projects without evidence. It does not let the LLM make irreversible financial decisions without explicit donor opt-in. It does not use images of suffering as conversion tools.

---

## Key Metrics

The product is healthy when: the Intention Tap to receipt flow completes in under 3 minutes for a new user; the allocation-optimizer edge function responds in under 2 seconds at the 95th percentile; every allocation plan is backed by at least one verified document chunk; the donor retention rate at six months exceeds 40%; and the Living Waqf Card serenity score for active assets averages above 70.
